# Figure Legend Checklist

Use for publication-readiness review of figure legends.

- Takeaway: the title or first sentence names the finding or figure purpose when supported.
- Stage: initial-submission legends may include a helpful takeaway; final legends follow journal condensation rules.
- Panel map: every panel letter is described in order.
- Main-text alignment: every panel cited in the text maps to the correct panel and claim.
- Visual definitions: colors, symbols, labels, abbreviations, and axes are defined.
- Conditions: controls, treatments, groups, time points, or sample types are identifiable.
- Evidence: the legend states what comparison the reader should inspect.
- Statistics: tests, thresholds, error bars, and sample sizes are included only when supplied.
- Self-containment: the legend can be understood without searching the main text.
- Scope: interpretation supports the figure but does not become a second results paragraph.
- Consistency: terminology matches the main text and figure labels.
- Missing information: absent figure details are flagged rather than invented.
- Reviewer inspection: a reader can inspect the figure without guessing comparison, condition, sample context, or statistical notation.
