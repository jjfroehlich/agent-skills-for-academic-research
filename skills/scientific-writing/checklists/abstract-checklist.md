# Abstract Checklist

Use for fast abstract review before returning a rewrite.

- Venue constraints: word limit, structured headings, and target audience are known or assumptions are stated.
- Move map: each sentence has a visible job: context, gap, approach, result/capability, evidence, or implication.
- Problem: the opening names a specific problem or tension, not a generic field fact.
- Gap or aim: the abstract explains what was unknown, limited, or needed.
- Approach: the method or design is described only at the level needed to understand the result.
- Result: the central finding, direction, or capability is explicit.
- Evidence: key evidence, validation, or comparison is present when available.
- Methods-paper variant: the unmet need, method name, primary innovation, capability, validation, and use case are visible.
- Searchability: important method names, systems, and comparison terms were not cut during compression.
- Interpretation: the final claim is bounded by the evidence.
- Brevity: background, citations, jargon, and duplicated method detail are cut.
- Standalone clarity: the abstract makes sense without the title or main text.
- Recoverability: a reader can reconstruct why the work was needed, what was done, what changed, and why it matters.
- Accuracy: no rewrite changes uncertainty, scope, causal strength, or statistical direction.
