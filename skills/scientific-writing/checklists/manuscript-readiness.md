# Manuscript Readiness Checklist

Use for whole-paper or section-level readiness review.

## Structure

- The manuscript has one visible main contribution or organizing question.
- Each major result connects to a question, hypothesis, prediction, or task.
- Section jobs are distinct: setup, reproducibility, evidence, interpretation, and implications are not blurred.
- Headings, topic sentences, and figure titles help readers recover the story.
- The order follows reader logic rather than lab chronology unless chronology is itself the result.
- Paragraphs follow context-content-conclusion or another clear topic/evidence/takeaway logic.
- Paragraphs have a topic sentence, support, and clincher or transition.
- Details that interrupt the main argument are moved to methods, supplement, appendix, or a later paragraph.

## Section Logic

- The introduction narrows from context to gap to aim.
- Results paragraphs state findings and evidence before broad interpretation.
- Results contain comparison, direction or qualitative pattern, evidence type, and figure/table reference when supplied.
- Figures or tables can be summarized as claims that advance the evidence spine.
- The discussion answers the research question and calibrates implications.
- Limitations name where results do and do not apply.
- Figures and legends are self-contained enough for inspection.
- Thesis chapters satisfy institutional structure and cross-chapter coherence.

## Claim Calibration

- Causal language matches the design.
- Scope, population, system, and uncertainty are explicit.
- Limitations bound interpretation instead of apologizing for the study.
- Future work follows from the contribution and does not undermine it.

## Prose

- Vague nouns and weak verbs are replaced where specificity improves accuracy.
- Long sentences are split when they hide actor, action, or claim.
- Base clauses are recoverable before modifiers and caveats are added.
- Comparable findings use parallel syntax unless a real scientific distinction requires different form.
- One term is used for one concept unless a distinction is intended.
- Jargon and abbreviations are defined only when needed.
- Style edits preserve meaning, caveats, and author voice.

## Authorship And Acknowledgements

- Contribution language names concrete roles.
- Acknowledgements do not imply hidden authorship.
- AI-assisted writing or analysis is disclosed in the appropriate section when required, and AI is not listed as an author.
- Private people or participants are named only when appropriate and permitted.
