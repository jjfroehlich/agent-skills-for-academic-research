# Publication Readiness Checklist

Use this before submission, preprint posting, presentation, or export-oriented advice.

## Scientific readiness

- The figure has one clear main claim or a staged multi-panel argument.
- The visual form matches the data type and does not hide observations, uncertainty, outliers, or sample structure.
- Statistical annotations are explained and do not replace effect size, interval, or replicate information.
- Representative images are backed by sample, replicate, or analysis-workflow context when the claim depends on generality.
- Specialized displays disclose ordering, clustering, transformations, thresholds, or scaling when those choices affect interpretation.

## Accessibility and interpretation

- Color choices match variable semantics and do not depend on hue alone.
- Labels, legends, abbreviations, units, symbols, channels, stains, insets, and annotations are defined.
- Scale bars have units wherever image interpretation requires scale.
- Panel labels and callouts are consistent and unambiguous.
- The figure can be interpreted at final size by readers who print, project, or view compressed PDFs.

## Layout and export

- Text, symbols, line weights, gridlines, and image details survive final column/page width, poster distance, or slide projection.
- Related panels align and share scales when comparison requires it.
- Raster panels have suitable final resolution; plots and diagrams remain vector when possible.
- Font embedding, file type, transparency, color mode, and panel assembly are checked against the target venue when known.
- Unknown venue requirements are listed as assumptions, not guessed.

## Final response template

```text
Readiness: ready / needs revision / blocked by missing info
Must fix before release:
1. <issue and fix>
2. <issue and fix>
Assumptions or checks still needed:
- <unknown venue, visual, or metadata requirement>
```
