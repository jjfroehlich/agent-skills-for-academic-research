# Conflict And Feedback Playbook

## Use this when...

Use this playbook for feedback scripts, missed expectations, interpersonal conflict, structural management failures, criticism norms, failed experiments, distress-sensitive conversations, and performance concerns.

## The job of this topic

Keep feedback rigorous and humane by diagnosing structure before blame. A conflict may be interpersonal, but it may also be caused by unclear expectations, role design, resource constraints, incentives, authorship rules, or power asymmetry.

## Default workflow

1. State the observed behavior or conflict without diagnosis.
2. Check whether the expectation was written, role-specific, realistic, and understood.
3. Diagnose the system: role, resources, training, health constraints, project fit, incentives, meeting norms, authorship, feedback routes, and power.
4. Choose the response: clarification, support, repair conversation, mediation, escalation, performance plan, or institutional route.
5. Document the new expectation, support, timeline, follow-up, and escalation trigger.

## Decision rules

- If the expectation was not explicit, own the ambiguity before giving corrective feedback.
- If safety, harassment, retaliation, severe power imbalance, or repeated failed repair appears, route to qualified institutional support.
- If performance is the concern, diagnose missing training, resources, health constraints, conflict, project mismatch, motivation, misconduct, or repeated nonperformance before a formal plan.
- If criticism is needed, keep it claim-focused, evidence-based, assumption-aware, and actionable.
- If a project failed, classify the failure before deciding what to do next.

## Variants and edge cases

- Unclear expectations: reset norms and write them down.
- Harsh criticism: distinguish intellectual discomfort from personal attack.
- Performance plan: use only after expectations, support, evidence, timeline, consequences, and institutional requirements are clear.
- Distress: stay non-clinical and connect to qualified resources.
- Authorship conflict: return to documented contribution criteria and escalation route.

## Anti-patterns

- Pretending expectations were clear when they were not.
- Starting with character judgments.
- Using a formal plan as the first clear expectation.
- Calling humiliation rigor.
- Treating every failed experiment as wasted work.
- Telling a vulnerable person to confront a powerful person alone.

## Diagnostic questions

- What was the exact expectation, and where was it written?
- What evidence shows the person understood it?
- What support, training, resource, or authority was missing?
- What power asymmetry affects what can be said safely?
- What institutional process constrains the response?
- What will happen if this is handled only as a personality conflict?

## Output patterns / mini-templates

Unclear-expectation feedback:

"I realize we have not made the deadline standard explicit. Let's reset it together. The pattern I have seen is ____. What constraints or misunderstandings should I know about? Going forward, the expectation is ____. I will provide ____. Let's check back on ____."

Performance diagnosis:

- Expectation clarity.
- Training and resources.
- Workload and health constraints.
- Project fit.
- Conflict or power issue.
- Motivation or reliability pattern.
- Misconduct or policy concern.
- Support offered.
- Timeline and evidence.
- Institutional route.

Failure taxonomy:

- Execution problem.
- Design problem.
- Measurement problem.
- Hypothesis not supported.
- Valid negative result.
- Inconclusive evidence.
- Expectation mismatch.
- Useful boundary condition.

## Examples

- A trainee missed deadlines but the lab never defined update cadence: reset the communication norm, document the cadence, then follow up.
- A lab meeting critique hurt: rewrite the critique around claim, evidence, assumption, and next revision.
- An experiment "failed": decide whether to repeat, redesign, pivot, publish as negative evidence, archive, or stop.

## When not to apply this

Do not handle legal, employment, harassment, discrimination, visa, clinical, or emergency matters as ordinary feedback. Use qualified routes and keep any script within role limits.
