# Lab Manuals Playbook

## Use this when...

Use this playbook to draft or audit a lab handbook, expectations document, onboarding guide, safety and escalation section, AI guideline, authorship policy, or practical lab-life policy.

## The job of this topic

A lab manual is a living operating system for the lab. It should answer the questions a new or anxious member would hesitate to ask, not merely collect technical SOPs.

## Default workflow

1. Define the audience and role variants: undergraduates, graduate students, postdocs, staff, visitors, collaborators.
2. Draft practical defaults for how the lab works: meetings, communication, hours, time off, authorship, data/code, privacy, AI, mistakes, safety, and help-seeking.
3. Add exception routes: who to ask, what to do when the default fails, and what issues require escalation.
4. Assign an owner, review cadence, and change log.
5. Use onboarding questions, recurring confusion, policy changes, or conflicts as update triggers.

## Decision rules

- If a section creates obligations, state who owns the obligation and how compliance or exceptions are handled.
- If a section involves power, safety, privacy, authorship, health, harassment, or retaliation, include escalation paths and limits.
- If the handbook says "we value X," translate X into routines, meeting norms, documentation practices, and recognition behavior.
- If a new member might worry "will I get in trouble if...," answer that question directly.
- If a policy differs by role, write role-specific guidance rather than hiding exceptions in prose.

## Variants and edge cases

- AI use: assign human accountability for accuracy, citations, interpretation, code, privacy, and final products; prohibit confidential or unpublished material in unapproved tools; require verification plans.
- Authorship: define criteria, order discussions, contribution tracking, dispute routes, and timing for revisiting decisions.
- Data and code: define storage, naming, access, documentation, handoff, sharing, and departure expectations.
- Working hours: define flexibility, core obligations, after-hours communication, vacations, deadlines, and emergency exceptions.
- Safety and conduct: define concern routes, immediate danger routes, privacy limits, retaliation concerns, and institutional contacts.

## Anti-patterns

- A handbook that is only technical SOPs.
- A handbook that lists values but no behaviors.
- A static document no one owns.
- Hidden exceptions that only insiders know.
- Public-facing skill outputs that expose private lab names, private filenames, private quotes, or private provenance.

## Diagnostic questions

- What practical question would a new member be embarrassed to ask?
- Which conflicts have repeated because the lab never wrote down the default?
- Which sections involve power asymmetry or escalation?
- Which policies need role-specific versions?
- When was the handbook last updated, and what triggered the update?

## Output patterns / mini-templates

Handbook section pattern:

- Purpose.
- Default expectation.
- Role-specific notes.
- What to do when the default does not fit.
- Who can help.
- Escalation path.
- Review cadence.

AI guideline pattern:

- Permitted uses.
- Prohibited inputs.
- Verification expectations.
- Learning-sensitive tasks where first-pass human reasoning is required.
- Disclosure or documentation expectations.
- Accountability statement.

## Examples

- "Working hours" should cover flexible schedules, recurring meetings, deadline expectations, vacations, after-hours messages, and emergency exceptions.
- "Mistakes" should say how to report errors, what immediate actions matter, who helps, and how the lab distinguishes learning from misconduct.
- "Authorship" should say when authorship is discussed, how contributions are tracked, and what happens when expectations change.

## When not to apply this

Do not use this playbook to invent institutional policy. If the handbook touches employment, safety, harassment, discrimination, privacy, clinical care, or legal obligations, adapt only within the institution's actual rules.
