# Poster Review Checklist

Use before printing, uploading, or presenting a scientific poster.

## Venue And Format

- Poster size, orientation, margin, and upload requirements are known.
- The design fits print, static digital, or interactive digital use.
- The poster is readable at standing distance or expected zoom level.
- Required logos, affiliations, acknowledgments, and contact details are present but not dominant.

## Main Message

- The title or top visual area signals the main finding, question, or takeaway.
- A passerby can understand the core claim without reading the full poster.
- The central evidence is visually dominant.
- Background and methods are only as detailed as the viewer needs.

## Layout And Reading Path

- The reading path is obvious.
- The poster uses a small number of visual blocks.
- Headings tell the story rather than merely naming IMRaD sections.
- Whitespace and grouping separate ideas.
- No text block looks like a manuscript paragraph pasted onto the poster.

## Figures And Text

- Paper figures have been redrawn, cropped, enlarged, or relabeled for poster viewing.
- Legends are shortened and labels are near the data.
- Abbreviations are limited or explained.
- Font sizes and contrast survive a distance check.
- QR-linked or handout material is used for optional detail instead of crowding the poster.

## Presenter Prep

- The presenter has a 30-second walkthrough.
- The presenter has a 3-minute walkthrough.
- Likely questions have concise answers.
- The poster supports conversation rather than trying to answer everything alone.

## Highest-Leverage Fixes

When time is short, prioritize in this order:

1. Rewrite the title or top claim.
2. Enlarge and clarify the main result.
3. Cut background and methods text.
4. Fix reading order, labels, and distance readability.
