# Specific Aims Checklist

Use this for aims-only reviews.

## Checks

- The objective is visible before the aims.
- The active premise explains what the aims collectively test, build, or prove.
- Each aim has a distinct purpose.
- The aim order is logical, but the proposal does not collapse if one aim is delayed or partially fails.
- Any unavoidable dependency is supported by preliminary data or an alternative path.
- An overview figure or verbal map shows relationships among aims.
- Each aim includes rationale, plan, feasibility evidence, interpretable outcomes, main risk, and backup.
- Preliminary data are selected for feasibility, not general productivity.
- The aims fit the funder's expected structure and page constraints.

## Red Flags

- Aim titles are only topic labels.
- Aim 2 and Aim 3 require Aim 1 to succeed with no fallback.
- More than three major aims are presented without a funder reason.
- Methods are listed without explaining decisions or outcomes.
- Completed work is framed as future funded work.
