# Grant Readiness Checklist

Use this for a fast proposal review before submission or before a deeper rewrite.

## Funder Fit And Compliance

- The opportunity funds this type of work.
- Current eligibility, location, career-stage, budget, and deadline rules are checked.
- Required headings, templates, page limits, formatting, and portal fields are followed.
- Each assessor criterion maps to a findable section or label.

## Narrative And Aims

- The objective appears early and is concise.
- The active premise connects the problem, aims, approach, and expected payoff.
- The first page makes the problem matter.
- Aims are independently valuable where possible.
- Aim dependencies are justified by preliminary data or backup paths.
- The project is grouped into a reviewer-manageable number of goals or aims.

## Significance, Innovation, And Approach

- Significance explains field state, unmet need, and why the work matters now.
- Innovation names what is new and why that novelty enables the work.
- Approach sections include rationale, plan, interpretable outcomes, risks, and alternatives.
- High-risk claims are paired with high return and concrete feasibility evidence.

## Feasibility And Budget

- Preliminary data or examples reduce specific reviewer concerns.
- Budget and allowed costs support the promised work.
- CV, team, environment, or institutional evidence proves execution capacity.
- Completed work is not presented as future funded work.
- Project-change or resubmission issues have a support plan.

## Reviewer Readability

- Headings, topic sentences, figures, and intentional emphasis form a coherent skim path.
- Figures are legible, aligned, high-resolution, and tied to claims.
- Non-specialist reviewers get enough context to score the proposal.
- Diverse critical readers have reviewed the draft early enough for revision.

## Failure Signals

- Out-of-scope project.
- Buried objective.
- Generic significance or novelty.
- Co-dependent aims with no support.
- Boilerplate impact, DEI, CV, or budget sections.
- Hidden risks or missing alternatives.
- Altered templates or hard-to-find compliance answers.
