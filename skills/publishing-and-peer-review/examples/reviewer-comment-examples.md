# Reviewer Comment And Response Examples

Use these as patterns, not canned text. Adapt them to the manuscript, journal, and decision letter.

## Constructive Major Comment

Weak:

```text
The mechanism is not convincing. More experiments are needed.
```

Stronger:

```text
The manuscript claims that [mechanism] explains [phenotype], but the current evidence shows association rather than mechanism. Please either add evidence that directly tests [mechanistic step] or narrow the claim to state that [observed association] is consistent with, but does not establish, [mechanism].
```

Why it works: it identifies claim, evidence gap, consequence, and bounded options.

## Reviewer Overreach Control

Weak:

```text
The authors should repeat the study in a new animal cohort.
```

Stronger:

```text
The organism-level claim is broader than the current cellular evidence. A new cohort would be one way to support that claim, but if that is outside the scope of the revision, the authors should narrow the conclusion and add a limitation that organism-level validation remains future work.
```

Why it works: it separates the legitimate concern from an open-ended experiment demand.

## Statistical Review Comment

```text
The manuscript compares responses in groups A and B, but the analysis appears to test each group separately rather than directly testing the difference between groups. Please provide a direct comparison aligned with the stated claim, or revise the claim so it does not imply a between-group difference.
```

Why it works: it targets a decision-relevant analysis issue without overclaiming expertise.

## Confidential Editor Note

```text
Confidential note to editor: My main concern is that the manuscript's central claim depends on [issue]. The authors may be able to address this by [bounded path], but without that change I do not think the current claim is supported. I have not included an accept/reject recommendation in the author-facing comments.
```

Why it works: it gives the editor decision-relevant synthesis without hiding feedback authors need.

## Response: Accepted Change

```text
Reviewer comment: [Comment]

Response: We agree. We revised [section/location] to clarify [specific point]. The revised text now states [brief paraphrase of change].
```

Why it works: it acknowledges, states the change, and gives a location.

## Response: Respectful Disagreement

```text
Reviewer comment: [Comment]

Response: We appreciate this concern. We have not added [requested experiment/analysis] because [scope, data, or feasibility reason]. To address the underlying issue, we revised [section/location] to narrow the claim and added [limitation/clarification].
```

Why it works: it does not pretend the work was done and still improves the manuscript.

## Response: Reviewer Misunderstanding

```text
Reviewer comment: [Comment]

Response: This comment showed us that the original wording was unclear. We revised [section/location] to distinguish [A] from [B] and added [clarifying phrase or sentence].
```

Why it works: it treats misunderstanding as a manuscript clarity problem rather than a reviewer failure.

## Response: Conflicting Reviewer Requests

```text
Reviewer 1 asked us to expand [topic], whereas Reviewer 2 asked us to shorten the same section. We addressed both concerns by adding one sentence that states [key clarification] and removing [speculative or redundant material]. This keeps the section concise while making the rationale explicit.
```

Why it works: it explains the tradeoff and the chosen revision path.
