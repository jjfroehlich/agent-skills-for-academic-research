# Submission Readiness Checklist

Use before first submission, transfer, resubmission after rejection, or appeal.

## Fit

- The manuscript's central claim is explicit.
- The target journal's scope and article type match the manuscript.
- The target audience needs the work as framed.
- Novelty or practical value is stated without exaggeration.
- The title, abstract, and cover letter point to the same contribution.

## Package Completeness

- Manuscript, figures, tables, supplemental files, and metadata are complete.
- Data, code, reporting, ethics, and competing-interest statements meet journal instructions.
- Author information and contribution statements are complete.
- Suggested or excluded reviewer information follows journal policy.
- File names, figure numbering, and supplementary references are consistent.

## Cover Letter

- The first paragraph names the article type, title, central claim, and fit.
- The letter explains why the journal's audience should care.
- Required declarations and prior process notes are included when relevant.
- The tone is concise, humble, and specific.
- The letter is addressed to the correct journal and editor or editorial office.

## Prior Decision Or Review History

- Rejection reasons are categorized before choosing appeal, transfer, or new submission.
- Valid reviewer concerns have been addressed before resubmission elsewhere.
- Any appeal is based on a specific decision-relevant error.
- Any prior review or transfer history is disclosed when appropriate.
- The new submission is not just the rejected package with a different cover letter.

## Final Risk Check

- What is the single most likely reason for desk rejection?
- What missing file or declaration could block technical acceptance?
- What claim could be narrowed to better match the evidence?
- What question should be sent to the editor before submission, if any?
