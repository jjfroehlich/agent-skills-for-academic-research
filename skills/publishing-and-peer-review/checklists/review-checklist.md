# Peer Review Checklist

Use before sending a review or when auditing a draft reviewer report.

## Accept Or Decline

- I have enough expertise to evaluate the central claim.
- I can meet the deadline without producing a rushed review.
- I have checked for conflicts, competition, prior involvement, or confidentiality issues.
- I know whether the journal wants confidential comments, a recommendation, or both.
- If declining, I can do so promptly and suggest expertise areas or names when appropriate.

## Manuscript Understanding

- The review begins with a neutral summary of the question, approach, and claim.
- The main contribution is identified before criticism begins.
- The review distinguishes journal fit from scientific validity.
- The review names what evidence would change the evaluation.

## Major Concerns

- Each major concern affects validity, interpretation, scope, reproducibility, ethics, or decision relevance.
- Each major concern includes evidence from the manuscript.
- Each major concern states the consequence for the claim.
- Each requested action is feasible within revision or explicitly framed as a claim-limit issue.
- Major comments are prioritized; the review does not bury the main problem.

## Statistical And Evidence Checks

- Comparisons are direct when the claim compares groups, effects, or conditions.
- Units of analysis and independence are appropriate.
- Sample size, uncertainty, assumptions, and multiplicity are not ignored.
- Figures and tables support the claims made from them.
- Conclusions do not overreach beyond the data.
- If statistical expertise is limited, the review says so and suggests specialist review.

## Minor Concerns

- Minor comments are separated from major comments.
- Minor comments cover clarity, figure presentation, terminology, or limited wording issues.
- Minor comments do not become disguised demands for new projects.

## Ethics And Tone

- Confidential or ethics concerns are routed to the editor-only channel.
- The review avoids sarcasm, personal criticism, and unsupported accusations.
- The wording would be acceptable if signed.
- The review does not ask for unnecessary citations, analyses, or experiments.
- The review protects confidentiality and unpublished information.

## Final Pass

- The editor can identify the decision-relevant issues quickly.
- The authors can tell what to change and why.
- The review is bounded to the manuscript's claims.
- The review is useful even if the recommendation is negative.
