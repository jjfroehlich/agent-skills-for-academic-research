# CV and Resume

Use this when the user needs an academic CV, industry resume, non-academic resume, LinkedIn-style summary, or a conversion between academic and non-academic materials.

## The job of this topic

Choose the right document genre and make the user's evidence legible to the intended reader. Academic CVs preserve a comprehensive scholarly record; resumes select and translate evidence for a specific role.

## Default workflow

1. Identify target role, sector, country or local norm, reader, page limit, and whether the artifact is a CV or resume.
2. Decide what the reader needs to learn first: research record, technical fit, teaching, leadership, product relevance, communication, or operations.
3. Reorder sections so the strongest relevant evidence appears early.
4. Rewrite bullets to show action, individual contribution, method or scope, result, and relevance.
5. Remove or compress details that do not serve the target reader.
6. Preserve a master CV separately from tailored exports.

## Decision rules

- If the reader is academic, keep comprehensive scholarly categories and make research, teaching, grants, mentoring, service, and publications easy to scan.
- If the reader is non-academic, prioritize relevance, brevity, role keywords, outcomes, collaboration, and evidence of transferable skills.
- If a bullet describes the lab but not the user, rewrite it around the user's action and contribution.
- If a section is impressive but not relevant to the role, move it later or compress it.
- If personal data, photos, or demographic details are culturally variable, check the local norm and avoid unnecessary disclosure.

## Variants and Edge Cases

- For PhD students, include training, methods, publications, presentations, teaching, outreach, and leadership only when relevant.
- For postdocs, foreground independence, outputs, methods, mentoring, funding, and field-building evidence.
- For industry resumes, translate publications into problems solved, techniques used, decisions made, stakeholders served, and outcomes.
- For faculty applications, route package-level strategy to `faculty-search.md` but use this file for CV mechanics.

## Anti-Patterns

- Sending a full academic CV to a role that asked for a resume.
- Listing every technique without showing judgment, scope, or result.
- Hiding the most relevant section under chronology that favors less relevant work.
- Inventing metrics or overstating impact.
- Maintaining only tailored versions and losing a complete source record.

## Diagnostic Questions

- Who will read this document, and what decision are they making?
- What three pieces of evidence should they see in the first third?
- Which bullets prove individual contribution rather than lab context?
- Which academic terms need translation for this sector?
- What should be cut, compressed, moved, or expanded?

## Output Patterns / Mini-Templates

Bullet rewrite pattern:

> Verb + individual contribution + method/scope + result + relevance.

Example transformation:

| Academic version | Targeted resume version |
|---|---|
| Worked on single-cell RNA-seq project in cancer lab | Analyzed single-cell RNA-seq datasets to identify tumor-cell states, producing reproducible workflows used by a cross-functional cancer biology team |

## Examples

- Academic CV section order may foreground education, research appointments, publications, grants, teaching, mentoring, presentations, service, and skills.
- Industry resume section order may foreground summary, technical skills, selected experience, projects, publications if relevant, and education.
- A master CV can keep every talk and award; a tailored CV or resume should select what supports the current opportunity.

## When not to apply this

Do not use this playbook for cover-letter persuasion or interview preparation except where the user needs document evidence first.
