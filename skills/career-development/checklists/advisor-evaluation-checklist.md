# Advisor Evaluation Checklist

Use this for a fast, evidence-based pass on advisor, mentor, or lab choice.

## Evidence to Gather

- Advisor access: meeting cadence, feedback mode, responsiveness, and availability during crises.
- Training structure: onboarding, project choice, skill development, writing support, and milestone planning.
- Credit and ownership: authorship norms, project portability, conference support, and public recognition.
- Funding and resources: stipend, contract duration, grant dependency, equipment, data access, and personnel.
- Trainee outcomes: completion, publications, next roles, time to degree or next job, and alumni willingness to talk.
- Lab climate: how people handle mistakes, disagreement, stress, boundaries, and career goals outside the advisor's preference.
- Fallbacks: alternative advisors, rotations, committee support, ombuds resources, and exit options.

## Questions to Ask

- How often do trainees meet with the advisor one-on-one?
- Can you describe a recent project that changed direction and how the advisor handled it?
- How are authorship and project ownership decided?
- What happens when a trainee is struggling or misses a milestone?
- What career paths has the advisor actively supported?
- Would former trainees choose this lab again?

## Triangulation Pass

- Ask current trainees at different stages.
- Ask recent alumni who no longer depend directly on the advisor.
- Ask adjacent students or staff who see the lab under pressure.
- Ask the advisor for concrete examples, then compare those examples with trainee accounts.
- Treat repeated independent reports as stronger evidence than one enthusiastic or fearful account.

## Red Flags

- Trainees seem afraid to answer ordinary questions.
- Success is described as depending on "thick skin" or managing the advisor.
- Authorship, funding, expectations, or project ownership are vague.
- Current and former trainees report retaliation, humiliation, credit theft, isolation, or ignored boundaries.
- The advisor dismisses the user's actual career goals.

## Decision Output

Return a table with `criterion`, `evidence`, `risk`, `missing evidence`, and `next action`. When risk is high, prioritize safety planning and neutral support over persuasion.
