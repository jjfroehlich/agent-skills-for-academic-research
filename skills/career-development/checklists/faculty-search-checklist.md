# Faculty Search Checklist

Use this before or during a faculty or group-leader search to audit readiness and package coherence.

## Readiness Audit

- Independent research identity in one sentence.
- Future program that is distinct from prior mentors.
- Evidence of execution: papers, methods, funding, collaborations, mentoring, or field-building.
- Fit targets by institution type, department needs, resources, and teaching expectations.
- Timeline for letters, statements, talks, visits, and references.
- Mentor or peer feedback on the package before submission.

## Package Coherence

- CV supports the research and teaching case.
- Cover letter explains specific institutional fit.
- Research statement shows future questions, feasibility, first projects, funding path, and local resource fit.
- Teaching or mentoring statement uses concrete evidence when requested.
- Diversity, service, or outreach material is included only when requested or expected and is evidence-based.

## Interview Preparation

- Job talk: clear problem, contribution, evidence, and future direction.
- Chalk talk: feasible first projects, alternatives, resources, personnel, and grant logic.
- One-on-ones: tailored questions for faculty, trainees, administrators, and collaborators.
- Difficult questions: independence, funding risk, failed projects, and fit tradeoffs.

## Offer and Startup Check

- People, salary, space, equipment, start date, moving support, teaching relief, protected time, and written terms.
- Must-haves versus tradeable items.
- Rationale for each ask tied to execution risk.

## Output

Return the top readiness gaps, the next package edit, the highest-risk interview issue, and the negotiation items to clarify if an offer emerges.
