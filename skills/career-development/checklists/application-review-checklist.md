# Application Review Checklist

Use this for CVs, resumes, cover letters, faculty materials, postdoc outreach, and other career artifacts.

## First Pass

- Identify the target role, reader, document type, stage, and deadline.
- State the reader's likely decision question in one sentence.
- Name the strongest evidence already present.
- Name the most important missing or buried evidence.
- Check whether the document follows the right genre for the audience.

## Evidence Check

- Does the opening third show the most relevant evidence?
- Are claims backed by outputs, actions, decisions, results, or examples?
- Does each bullet show the user's contribution rather than only lab context?
- Are technical terms translated when the reader is non-academic?
- Are metrics honest and supplied by the user?

## Structure Check

- Academic CV: comprehensive scholarly record, easy section scanning, complete chronology where expected.
- Industry resume: selected evidence, role language, concise bullets, relevant skills, and outcomes.
- Cover letter: specific role, criteria match, evidence, fit, and concise close.
- Faculty package: coherent research identity, future program, institutional fit, and resources needed.

## Bullet Rewrite Pattern

Use `action + individual contribution + method or scope + result + relevance`.

Avoid inventing metrics. If no result is supplied, ask for one or use a truthful scope, output, or decision.

## Cover Letter Final Pass

- Uses the reader's language accurately.
- Names the specific role, lab, department, or organization.
- Gives concrete examples rather than adjectives.
- Explains fit instead of repeating the CV.
- Has a concise close and no generic opening paragraph.

## Output

Return the top three fixes in priority order, then provide targeted rewrites only for sections that affect the reader's decision.
