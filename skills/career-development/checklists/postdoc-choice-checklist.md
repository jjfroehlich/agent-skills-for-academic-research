# Postdoc Choice Checklist

Use this when the user is comparing postdoc labs, PI conversations, or offers.

## Career Purpose

- What the postdoc must accomplish: new field, new method, publications, independence, funding, faculty readiness, industry bridge, or geographic constraint.
- What evidence the user needs for the next role.
- What would make the postdoc a success after one, two, and three years.

## Lab and Project Evidence

- Project ownership and intellectual independence.
- Publication prospects and authorship norms.
- Funding duration and contingency plans.
- Mentor investment, meeting cadence, and feedback style.
- Resources: data, equipment, organisms, collaborators, staff, and technical support.
- Alumni outcomes, including people with the user's target career path.
- Whether the project can create a portable niche.

## Questions to Ask

- What will I own intellectually?
- How are papers, authorship, and project pivots handled?
- What happens if the first project fails?
- How do postdocs prepare for fellowships, faculty searches, or non-academic moves?
- Which recent postdocs reached roles like the one I want?

## Risk Flags

- The project depends on someone else's unfinished work.
- Authorship, funding, or project portability is vague.
- The lab's prestige is stronger than the evidence of support.
- The PI cannot describe how postdocs build independence.
- The postdoc delays a transition without adding target-role evidence.

## Output

Return a comparison table with `lab`, `career purpose`, `ownership`, `support evidence`, `resources`, `risk flags`, and `next action`.
