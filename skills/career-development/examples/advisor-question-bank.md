# Advisor Question Bank

Use these patterns for visit days, rotations, postdoc interviews, and mentor-comparison calls. Adapt the wording to the user's stage and safety constraints.

## Project Choice

- How are new projects chosen in the lab?
- Can you describe a recent project that changed direction and how that decision was made?
- What does a trainee own independently, and what stays shared with the lab or collaborators?

## Feedback and Meetings

- How often do trainees meet one-on-one with the advisor?
- What does feedback look like on experiments, writing, and career planning?
- How does the advisor handle a trainee who is stuck or behind?

## Authorship and Credit

- How are authorship and author order decided?
- Can you give an example of how a trainee's contribution was recognized?
- What happens when a project involves multiple trainees or collaborators?

## Funding and Resources

- How long is funding secure, and what happens if a grant changes?
- Are there resources, equipment, data, or personnel that the project depends on?
- What support exists for fellowships, conferences, or training?

## Career Support

- What career paths has the advisor actively supported?
- How does the advisor respond when a trainee wants a non-academic path?
- Which alumni would be useful to contact, and would they be willing to talk?

## Conflict and Safety

- What happens when a trainee disagrees with the advisor?
- Who can a trainee talk to if the advisor relationship is not working?
- Can you describe a time when expectations were unclear and how they were resolved?

## Output Pattern

Group questions by audience:

| Audience | Best questions | Evidence sought | Risk if unanswered |
|---|---|---|---|
| Advisor | Project ownership, feedback, funding | Process and commitments | Promises stay vague |
| Current trainees | Normal week, stress, conflict | Lived behavior | Only official story heard |
| Alumni | Would choose again, career support | Outcomes after dependency | Current-trainee bias remains |
