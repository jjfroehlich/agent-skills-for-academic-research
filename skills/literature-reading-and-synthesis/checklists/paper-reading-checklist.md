# Paper Reading Checklist

Use before presenting, discussing, or relying on a paper.

- State the reading goal and deadline.
- Classify the article type.
- Choose triage, targeted, evidence, or deep-reading depth.
- Identify sections that matter for the goal.
- Answer the six questions: why, what, why this way, what found, author interpretation, what next.
- List key figures or tables to unpack.
- Record methods or assumptions that affect interpretation.
- Record limitations, alternatives, bias, confounders, and generalizability.
- Mark terms, supplemental material, or citations needing follow-up.
- Add a build-on-it note: what this changes and next action.
