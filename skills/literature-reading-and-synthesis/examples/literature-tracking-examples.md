# Literature Tracking Examples

## Starter Stream

Use a deliberately small portfolio:

| Source type | Role | Volume target | Action |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Topic query | Find directly relevant papers | Processable weekly stream | Tune after two weeks |
| High-value venue feed | Maintain field awareness | Small | Skim weekly |
| Review source | Refresh orientation | Occasional | Save selectively |

Do not expand until the current stream is being scanned consistently.

## Alert Overload

Problem: the queue is full of irrelevant papers and saved items are never read.

Repair:

1. Sort feeds by role and weekly volume.
2. Pause sources without a clear role.
3. Narrow common-term queries.
4. Add a backlog review date.
5. Keep only items with an explicit next action.

## Field Source Map

| Layer | Purpose | Example source type | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Broad field | Awareness | Major venue or review source | Too much irrelevant volume |
| Specialty topic | Relevance | Database query | Query drift |
| Method/tool | Implementation | Method venue or keyword feed | Missing biological context |
| Author/lab | Known group tracking | Author alert | Too narrow |
| Informal discovery | Early signal | Blog, social, preprint mention | Requires verification |
